Pasar Dewata

Pasar Dewata is a digital fantasy environment that is inspired by the vibrant cultural atmosphere of Ubud’s creative markets in Bali. This world blends traditional Balinese architecture, mythology, and spiritual philosophy into a richly designed market-scape, where the boundary between the physical and metaphysical worlds is intentionally blurred. While Balinese Hinduism recognises a wide pantheon of deities, the core of my world centres around two mythological figures: Barong and Rangda. Barong, the lion-shaped king of spirits, represents protection, goodness, and harmony. Rangda, the terrifying demon queen and leader of the Leyaks (evil spirits), symbolises chaos and destruction. They exist in eternal opposition. This symbolic battle between the two is most famously expressed through the Barong Dance, a traditional performance in Bali that tells the story of their cosmic struggle. In Pasar Dewata, these two beings observe the mortal world from afar, Barong seeking to protect and preserve, Rangda striving to corrupt and unbalance. To keep harmony in the realm, the people of the market perform the Barong Dance every night as an offering. It’s more than just a performance, it’s a vital ritual that pays tribute to Barong and keeps the balance between good and evil in check.

RANGDA & BARONG

These two were the main highlights of the virtual world. These two figures are central to the mythology explored in Pasar Dewata, representing the eternal cosmic struggle between good and evil. This project allowed me to dive deep into character design and rigging, bringing these iconic Balinese mythological beings to life in 3D.

"You've got a friend in me"

This project centers on two beloved characters from my childhood: BMO from Adventure Time and the Dispenser from Team Fortress 2. When I first became confident in 3D modeling using Blender, I immediately challenged myself to recreate these characters. Looking back, this render still holds a special place in my heart. It evokes feelings of nostalgia, comfort, playfulness, and a time when life felt fearless. Hence, I titled "You’ve Got a Friend in Me".
